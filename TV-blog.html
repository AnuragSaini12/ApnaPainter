TRANSFORM YOUR LIVING ROOM WITH THE PERFECT TV WALL COLOUR

Winters call for a cup of hot chocolate, slouching inside a comfy blanket, and binge-watching your favourite TV shows. Isn’t that the most fun part about the cold weather? But to add to the cozy feeling, a warm space would be so much better. The right TV wall paint colour ideas can help make that happen on time.

Your private space is one where you are most likely to spend maximum time during the Winter season. So, why not leverage it by amping it up properly? If you are someone who loves watching television and Christmas movies during the Winter, the right TV wall can help offer the perfect cinematic experience you need. Here’s all you desire to know about the same!

Creamy White TV Wall Paint Colour Ideas

White may be slightly tough to maintain, but it can be the most beautiful colour to incorporate into your TV wall. White colours can elevate the impression of your home. It may allow the walls to look larger and classier and provide a peaceful backdrop with no disturbance. But Creamy White is an even better pick for your TV wall paint ideas.

Mustard Yellow TV Wall Paint Colour Ideas

Are you already bored of the monotonous colour palettes that include Green, Brown, or Yellow for your living room? Well, it's time to aggravate your colour tones a bit. mustard colour is a timeless pick for those looking to add a pint of quirk and coziness to their living area. This TV wall colour design can soon elevate the undertones of your home and make it look inviting for all guests.

Shades Of Gray TV Wall Paint Colour Design

We generally associate Gray with dullness. But did you know that the right kind of Grays can be the perfect way to maximize liveliness in your living area? Infusing different shades of Gray together into a single space can be a great way to play with creativity. You can further deck them up with the right wall art to improve elegance, class, and drama in your personal space. They can work very well as a great TV backdrop too.

Rich Red TV Wall Paint Colour Ideas

Red is a luxurious colour for most homes. But so many of us find it a risky affair to incorporate such a bold colour into our private spaces. We are afraid we might go wrong by going all out to select such a royal colour for our homes. But why underestimate the power of our beautiful homes? Why not experiment a little to see just how much drama the right red can add to our TV wall paint ideas?

Don’t be afraid to make a statement by choosing a rich red background for your TV wall. This deep Red wall colour will not only help accessorize your living space without adding too many decor pieces. It can also help entice lots of guests to your home and be all praises for it. Do not forget to install a Chandelier as a centerpiece in your living home to complete the look. We promise; you can never go wrong with this pair!

A Simple Geometry Trick as Wall Stencil Designs :

If you’re wondering what to do with your bare wall, we have a solution. This trending geometry wall stencil design can add dimension and character to your wall. Just use a wall stencil to draw random lines across your wall, making it a larger wall art design. This not only creates a masterpiece in your living room, but also adds colour to your space.

Popular Tribal Feathers as Wall Stencil in Living Rooms :

Looking for a bohemian, tribal accent in your living room? This trend is what you need to go after. Intricate tribal feather wall stencils can be painted across a single wall in your living room to add a rustic and earthy touch. You can either use wall paint of the same family or create a contrasting visual with this design. And that is how you can have a boho-chic living room.

Moroccan Tile Designs Used As Wall Stencils in Living Rooms :

Don’t worry if you have never stepped foot in Morocco! Let’s bring the middle eastern royal touch to your living room walls. Use wall stencils that recreate Marhaba or Ikat-inspired Moroccan tile designs as wall stencils in your living room, to create a rich and sophisticated space. This is also a cost-effective way to save yourself from the expenses of tiling or panelling.
