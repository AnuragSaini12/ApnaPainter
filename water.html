4. How long does the waterproofing process task?

The duration of the waterproofing process for a home can vary depending on several factors, including the size of the property, the extent of the waterproofing required, and the specific techniques and materials used. Typically, the process can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to complete. The initial step in the waterproofing process involves a thorough inspection of the property to identify areas that require attention. This assessment helps determine the scope of work and the specific waterproofing methods needed. Once the assessment is complete, the actual waterproofing work can begin.

5. How long does waterproofing last?

The longevity of waterproofing largely depends on various factors such as the type of waterproofing material used, the quality of application, environmental conditions, and maintenance. Generally, well-applied waterproofing can last anywhere from 5 to 20 years. For example, liquid-based waterproofing coatings typically have a lifespan of 5 to 10 years, while bituminous membranes can last up to 15 years. However, it's important to note that these estimates are approximate, and certain factors like extreme weather conditions or inadequate maintenance can reduce their effectiveness and lifespan. Regular inspections, prompt repairs, and proper maintenance can help extend the lifespan of waterproofing systems..
